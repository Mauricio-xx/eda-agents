## Headline

**NEUTRAL verdict.** Skill injection neither helps nor hurts the
autoresearch loop on fazyrv-hachure frv_1. Both conditions explore
an identical proposal trajectory and land on an identical FoM. The
only measurable difference is prompt size (2.57× tokens ON).

## Setup

- Task: `e2e_digital_autoresearch_fazyrv_live` (new in this commit).
  Design space 5×4 = 20 combos, budget 4.
- Design: `FazyRvHachureDesign(macro="frv_1")` via the new
  `design_class: fazyrv_hachure` adapter dispatch. LibreLane lives
  inside fazyrv's nix-shell; invoked through
  `shell_wrapper = "nix-shell /home/.../gf180mcu-fazyrv-hachure --run"`.
- Relevant skills: `["digital.synthesis", "digital.physical",
  "digital.signoff"]` — three entries versus counter's two.
- Infra carve-out: the upstream leo/gf180mcu LibreLane pin has a
  broken KLayout DRC deck (resolves to `None`, fails at step 65).
  `FazyRvHachureDesign.librelane_extra_flags()` now returns
  `["--skip", "KLayout.DRC", "--skip", "Magic.DRC"]`, mirroring what
  fazyrv's own `make macro-nodrc` target does. Flow still runs CTS
  + routing + STA post-PnR; we lose DRC signoff as a validity knob
  but `DigitalDesign.check_validity()` on fazyrv keys off WNS, not
  DRC, so the audit stays meaningful.

## Exploration trajectory (identical in every run)

| eval | density | clock ns | WNS ns | FoM   | status     |
|------|---------|----------|--------|-------|------------|
| 1    | 65      | 40       | 6.00   | 13.75 | kept       |
| 2    | 85      | 40       | 8.45   | 16.11 | kept       |
| 3    | 85      | 35       | 3.45   | 10.40 | discarded  |
| 4    | 85      | 45       | 13.45  | 21.82 | kept (best)|

Six independent runs — three ON, three OFF — produce the same
sequence at the same eval numbers. Either Gemini's greedy posterior
is deterministic enough at temperature 0.7 on this prompt size, or
OpenRouter is caching identical (system_prompt, user_prompt) pairs
across the rapid re-run cadence. Either way, the effective sample
size on trajectory is N=1 per arm, not N=3.

## Why the null result is consistent with prior evidence

The counter-live A/B (`s10c_20260416T185823Z_analysis.md`) showed
the same pattern — skills ON and OFF converge to the same final
design. The Miller OTA one-shot A/B (`s10g_20260416T195409Z_analysis.md`)
was the outlier with +10 % Pass@1. The emerging rule: *skill
injection bites when the LLM must pick a single good design
without iteration; it goes silent when a multi-step greedy search
has enough budget to reach the ceiling regardless of priors.*

For the counter (12-combo space, budget 2) and fazyrv frv_1
(20-combo space, budget 4), the search converges on the same best
point by construction. For the Miller one-shot (one prompt → 5
sizing knobs → audit), the prior steered the proposal directly.

## Budget

Tokens ratio is 2.57× (12961 ON vs 5049 OFF per run, summed across
4 proposals per run). That's lower than Miller's 11.4× because the
digital user-prompt is dominated by flow-metric history, not a tiny
spec YAML; the skill prefix is a smaller fraction of the total.
4 324 tokens of skill content per proposal — well under the 12 k
soft cap in `render_relevant_skills`. No action needed.

## What this bench DID prove

- **S10h wiring works end-to-end**: adapter dispatch on
  `design_class`, `FazyRvHachureDesign.librelane_extra_flags()`,
  nix-shell wrapping, DRC skip workaround — all live on a real
  RISC-V macro.
- **No regression on the RISC-V path** when skills are injected.
- **Token instrumentation (S10e)** measures the prompt-bloat
  cleanly at 2.57× for digital skill bundles.

## What this bench did NOT prove

- That injecting `digital.{synthesis,physical,signoff}` improves
  LLM-driven autoresearch on a realistic design. The null result
  is real but does not rule out the hypothesis; it only rules out
  the hypothesis *at budget 4 on a 20-combo space*. A harder
  setup — larger macro, tighter specs, or a budget where the
  ceiling isn't reached in one sweep — is needed to re-test.

## Decision

Keep `EDA_AGENTS_INJECT_SKILLS` default at `"1"`. No ± 5 % gate
violated. Combined with S10c (digital counter: neutral), S10g
(Miller analog: +10 % Pass@1), and S10h (fazyrv RISC-V: neutral),
the arc ship-status is unchanged: positively supported where the
experiment has resolution, no regression where it doesn't.

## Follow-on (not in this commit)

- **Harder fazyrv variant**: try `chip-top` (3h16m per run per
  Phase 0) with tighter timing specs, budget 8. Much costlier.
- **N=10+ seeds** to bound the variance properly if trajectory
  non-determinism can be introduced (higher temperature, or a
  different provider that doesn't cache).
- **Tighter validity rule**: gate on `WNS > 1 ns` instead of the
  default `>= 0`. Would force the LLM to discriminate between
  configs rather than accept anything that closes.
- **Fix the upstream KLayout DRC deck wiring** in the
  leo/gf180mcu LibreLane pin so we can actually audit DRC on
  fazyrv — the skip is a workaround, not a fix.
